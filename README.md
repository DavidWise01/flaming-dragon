The company can email you but you can't email it; the gap between CAN-SPAM and ADA is where the customer falls through. Field-tested vs 60+ companies Dec 2025–Mar 2026, same result, because the defect is a vendor DEFAULT (Mailchimp/Constant Contact/Salesforce Marketing Cloud) not a per-company choice. Lands UNEQUALLY: phone-only excludes Deaf/HoH/speech-disabled, chat/CAPTCHA excludes screen-reader users, timed forms exclude cognitive/motor — and the human who used to catch the exception is now an automated layer that routes it nowhere (disparate impact). ⚑⚑ HONEST LEGAL FRAME (the manual's first draft got this WRONG, corrected here): it is NOT a damages goldmine — **ADA Title III gives private plaintiffs INJUNCTIVE RELIEF + attorneys' fees, NOT money damages**; the '$75k–$200k per incident' figures are the DOJ's CIVIL PENALTIES in government pattern-or-practice suits, payable to the U.S. Treasury, not to you — so any 'scale to 10,000 targets / license to plaintiff firms / $1M-a-year' model rests on a category error (dropped from this edition along with the per-company 'violation' accusations). Goal = compliance, not extraction. ⚑ THE FIX (it's a default, defaults change): vendors change the no-reply default to a monitored reply path or surface an accessible accommodation route; companies accept replies or label an accessible channel; the customer documents and seeks access (not a settlement).
